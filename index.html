A PASSIONATE IT, UX, PRODUCT MGT LEADER

I have been told on more than one occasion in my career skill sets such as entrepreneurship, innovation, disruption, and servant leadership can only be cultivated in start-ups; free from the bureaucratic processes of large traditional organizations. These are not skill sets. They are a mind set. They come from the soul. They are a result of one's passion and desire to invert traditional thinking on its head to get things done.

Passion is what allows me to think creatively regardless of my working environment. Passion enables me to embrace change (one of the very few constants in life) by choice. Passion pushes me to pursue lifelong learning to always be improving my IT, UX, and product management skills. Passion drives me to take risks, reap the rewards, learn from my failures, and lead by putting people and teams first.

Leadership Highlights

Senior Manager, Rapid Prototype Design Unit, Enterprise Technology Delivery, GovTechON

Our Mission is to offer a low-code service to empower all to rapidly prototype their own apps. Low code simplifies the development process by automating manual processes and makes it easier than ever to create flexible apps to get specific tasks done.

Our Vision is to drive low code adoption within the OPS through an established guide community of practice. This community will foster IT agility within the enterprise, manage evolving customer digital expectations, reduce shadow IT, and increase the opportunity for innovation.

Manager: Product Management & Design Chapter Manager, LTC Digital Garage

Digital Garage's mandate is to foster the practice of innovation and exploration of technology to drive the digital service transformation agenda in Ministry of Transportation and Ministry of Labour, Training and Skills Development.

As manager of the product management and design chapters, it was my responsibility to ensure that our teams have the methods, tools, resources and managerial support to be successful. When managing teams of talented individuals, I strived to support by servant leadership; that is, I listen and value diverse opinions, cultivate a culture of trust and comradeship, invest in developing the leadership qualities of others, persuade not command, and above all, act with a sense of humility.

Manager: Solutions Delivery, LTC .NET Solutions Delivery Centre

Managed at its peak a 70 strong development team for the Solutions Delivery Office, .NET SDC.

Using agile and human centred design methodologies, coupled with collaborative partnerships, the solutions delivery office within the .NET SDC delivers products and services that enable the legislative mandates of our ministries, and cluster clients.

The office provided effective, efficient and usable solutions while pursuing innovative, disruptive solutions and iterative (agile) improvements.

Manager, Human Centred Design Office, LTC .NET Solutions Delivery Centre

The long term goal of this practice was to create a culture of usability through human centred design to continuously improve the efficiency, effectiveness, and satisfaction of products and services for Ontario citizens.

As manager of this new initiative within the LTC .NET Solutions Delivery Centre, I was tasked with standing up and maturing this practice. Responsibilities included defining staff job roles, recruitment and training, defining product and services, processes, methods and tools, establishing intra-governmental and community partnerships, and UX evangelism.
